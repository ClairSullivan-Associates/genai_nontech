## Plain text CRAFT prompt

```
I am a [your role] and I need to respond to [a frustrated / an angry / a disappointed] [client / partner / stakeholder] email today. We were supposed to [deliver / complete / provide] [specific commitment] by [original deadline] and [what actually happened, e.g. it was delayed, it was incomplete, it went wrong]. The issue was caused by [brief honest description of root cause]. As a result, [describe the specific impact on the other party]. [Contact name and title] sent a [describe tone, e.g. professional but clearly frustrated] email asking for [what they want, e.g. an explanation, an apology, assurance this won't happen again]. [Any relationship context worth noting, e.g. renewal coming up, long-standing account, high visibility].

Act as a senior [customer success professional / account manager / communications expert] who is skilled at turning damaged [client / stakeholder / partner] relationships around through honest, confident communication.

Write a response email to [contact name] that takes full accountability without making excuses, acknowledges the specific impact our mistake had on [them / their team / their organization], and gives two or three concrete steps we are taking to prevent this from happening again. Keep the tone accountable and solution-focused, not groveling and not defensive. Keep it to four paragraphs or less and write it like something a real person would actually send, with no corporate fluff and no hollow apologies.

If there are multiple ways to phrase the accountability section, give me two options for that paragraph so I can choose the tone that fits my relationship with this person best.
```
