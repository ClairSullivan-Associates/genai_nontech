## Plain text CRAFT prompt

```
I need to prepare a [type of update: status update / briefing / summary] for [recipient and their role] who needs a clear picture of [topic or initiative] ahead of [deadline or event]. I'm pasting the full [communication source: email thread / Slack channel / meeting notes / chat log] below.

You are a senior [analyst / communicator / subject matter expert] who is skilled at cutting through messy, multi-voice communication threads and extracting what actually matters for a [executive / decision maker / stakeholder] audience.

Please work through the following questions based only on what appears in the thread: [Key question 1 relevant to your topic, e.g. status, progress, timeline], [Key question 2, e.g. resources, budget, capacity], [Key question 3, e.g. blockers, risks, open issues], and [Key question 4, e.g. overall health, readiness, sentiment]. Close with 3 to 5 bullet points the recipient could read in 60 seconds or less, and adjust the tone to match the audience. For a senior executive, be direct and concise. For a peer or working team, you can include slightly more detail. If the thread is technical, translate jargon into plain language.

One important rule: do not fill in gaps or make assumptions. If the thread does not clearly answer one of the questions, flag it as "unclear from available information." I would rather know what we don't know than receive a confident-sounding answer that isn't grounded in the source material.

[paste your communication thread here]
```
