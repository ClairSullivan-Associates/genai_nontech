## CRAFT prompt

```
C - Context
I'm preparing a status update for my boss who has a board meeting Monday.  She needs a clear, accurate picture of Project X, which is supposed to launch by end of February.  I'm pasting the full email thread below.
R - Role
You are a senior project analyst who specializes in translating messy communication threads into clean executive briefings.
A - Avoid
Do not include background noise, pleasantries, or side conversations that aren't relevant to project status.  Do not editorialize or add opinions not supported by the thread.  Do not assume information that isn't explicitly stated.
F - Format
Structure your response around these four questions...

1. Schedule status...are we on track, ahead, or behind?  What is the current launch date?
2. Budget status...are we over or under?
3. Current blockers or issues that need attention
4. Overall project health...is everything ready?

Close with 3 to 5 bullet points that my boss could read in 60 seconds.
T - Tweak
If the thread doesn't clearly answer one of the four questions, flag it as "unclear from available information" rather than guessing.
[paste entire email thread here]
```

## Plain text CRAFT prompt

```
I'm putting together a status update for my boss who has a board meeting Monday.  She needs a clear picture of Project X, which is supposed to launch by end of February.  I'm pasting the full email thread below.
You're a senior project analyst who's great at cutting through messy email threads and pulling out what actually matters for an executive audience.
Please work through these four questions...

1. Schedule status...are we on track, ahead, or behind?  What is the current launch date?
2. Budget status...are we over or under?
3. Current blockers or issues that need attention?
4. Overall project health...is everything ready to go?

Close with 3 to 5 bullet points my boss could read in 60 seconds flat.
One important thing...if the thread doesn't clearly answer one of those questions, just flag it as "unclear from available information" rather than filling in the gaps.  I'd rather know what we don't know than get a confident-sounding answer that isn't actually in the thread.
[paste entire email thread here]
```
