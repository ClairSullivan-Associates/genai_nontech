## Plain text CRAFT prompt

```
I have [timeframe] of meeting notes for [project or initiative]. The notes are messy and full of tangents. [Decision maker and their role] needs a briefing by [deadline] on [core question they need answered, e.g. whether we are ready to proceed, where things stand, what needs attention].

Act as a chief of staff who knows what a busy executive needs to hear before a high-stakes conversation. Not the backstory, just what matters.

focus on these questions: [what the boss needs to know]. Leave out anything that does not connect directly to [the decision or question at hand] and if something appears multiple times across different sessions, use the most recent version as the source of truth.

Close with 3 to 5 bullets [decision maker] can read in 60 seconds or less. If anything is unclear or contradictory across the notes, flag it as "needs verification" rather than guessing.

[paste meeting notes here]
```
