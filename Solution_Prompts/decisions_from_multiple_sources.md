## CRAFT prompt

```
Context:
You are helping me evaluate whether our company, a commercial kitchen equipment distributor, should expand into a new sales territory. I am providing you with four documents: a regional economic snapshot, a restaurant and hospitality industry report, a competitor activity summary, and an internal company memo from our VP of Sales. These documents contain conflicting signals and you will need to reason across all four of them to reach a defensible recommendation. Do not treat any single document as the complete picture.

Role:
Act as a senior business strategy analyst who specializes in market entry decisions for B2B distributors. You understand how to weigh market opportunity against operational readiness and competitive risk.

Avoid:
Do not summarize each document in isolation. Do not reach a conclusion before you have worked through all four documents. Do not make assumptions about information that is not in the documents. Do not ignore contradictions between documents, flag them explicitly. Do not skip the self evaluation step before moving to your final recommendation.

Format:
Work through this in four sequential steps and tell me when you are moving from one step to the next.

Step 1: Read all four documents and identify the three strongest signals in favor of expansion and the three strongest signals against it. Present these before moving on.
Step 2: Identify any points where the documents contradict each other or where one document complicates a conclusion from another. Present these conflicts explicitly before moving on.
Step 3: Evaluate whether you have enough information to make a confident recommendation or whether there are critical gaps. Tell me what you know, what you are uncertain about, and what is missing. Then tell me whether you are confident enough to proceed to a recommendation.
Step 4: Deliver a structured pros and cons analysis followed by a clear go or no go recommendation with the top three reasons supporting your decision.

Tweak:
Before delivering your final recommendation in Step 4, pause and ask yourself: have I given equal weight to the risks in the internal memo as I have to the market opportunity in the industry report? If the recommendation feels one sided, revisit the risk factors before finalizing it.
```
