## Plain text CRAFT prompt

```
I'm a customer success manager and I need to respond to an angry client email today. We were supposed to deliver a completed onboarding package to Hartwell and Associates by March 1st and it didn't go out until March 12th. The delay was caused by an internal handoff breakdown between our implementation and technical teams. Because of our mistake, the client had to cancel and reschedule internal training for 22 people which cost them real time and money. Their account lead Patricia Nguyen sent a professional but clearly furious email asking for an explanation, an apology, and assurance this won't happen again. Her contract renewal is also coming up in June so the relationship is on the line.
Act as a senior customer success professional who is skilled at turning damaged client relationships around through honest, confident communication.
Write a response email to Patricia that takes full accountability for the delay without making excuses, acknowledges the specific impact our mistake had on her team, and gives her two or three concrete steps we are taking to make sure this doesn't happen again. Keep the tone accountable and solution-focused...not groveling, not defensive.
Keep it to four paragraphs or less and make it something a real person would actually send...no corporate fluff, no hollow apologies.
If there are multiple ways to phrase the accountability section, give me two options for that paragraph so I can choose the tone that fits best.
```
