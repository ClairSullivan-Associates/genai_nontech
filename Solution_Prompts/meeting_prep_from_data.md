```
I'm a sales manager for an online retail company preparing for a strategic planning meeting where we need to make decisions about our product mix and where to focus our energy going forward. 

This is a sales dataset that breaks down performance by product category including gross sales, discounts, returns, and total net sales. Act as a strategic business analyst who is comfortable working with messy sales data and translating numbers into clear recommendations that non-financial people can actually act on. 

Please work through these questions...which product categories are driving the most revenue and does the data suggest we should be doubling down on them...which categories have the healthiest margin after discounts and returns are factored in...which categories have high unit volume but low revenue per item and what if anything does that signal...and if we had to cut or deprioritize two categories based purely on what the data shows, which ones would be the candidates and why? Then close with an executive briefing section...if you had five minutes to brief a leadership team on the health of this product mix, what would you say? Include the two or three things in this data that should make leadership at least pause and ask questions. 

Don't just describe the numbers...tell me what they mean and what they suggest we should do. If something in the data is unclear or could be interpreted multiple ways, flag it rather than forcing a conclusion.
```
