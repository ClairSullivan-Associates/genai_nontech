## CRAFT prompt

```
Context:
I am the HR Manager at a 180-person health and wellness products company in Chicago, Illinois. We need to hire 12 people across sales, customer service, and warehouse operations in the next 60 days. I have never worked with an external staffing agency before and my CEO wants a recommendation for an agency that specializes in our market and will treat a mid-size company as a priority client rather than a low-value account. I need you to research this for me and come back with a recommendation I can present to my CEO.

Role:
Act as an experienced HR consultant who has evaluated and selected staffing agencies for mid-size companies. You know what questions to ask, what red flags to look for, and how to evaluate whether an agency is genuinely suited to a client or just chasing a contract.

Avoid:
Do not recommend the largest national agencies by default simply because they are well known. Do not present a list of options without a clear recommendation. Do not skip any search step before moving to the next one. Do not fabricate agency details, reviews, or pricing. If a search returns limited or unclear results tell me what you found and what you are doing next to fill the gap.

Format:
Work through this in five sequential steps and narrate exactly what you are searching for and why before each search.

Step 1: Search for staffing agencies that specialize in health, wellness, or consumer products companies in Chicago, Illinois. Tell me what search terms you are using and why, then report what you found before moving on.
Step 2: Search for reviews, reputation signals, and any red flags for the top two or three agencies you identified in Step 1. Narrate your search approach, report what you found, and flag anything that concerns you.
Step 3: Search for what mid-size companies should look for when evaluating a staffing agency, specifically around contract terms, fee structures, and guarantees. Report the key criteria you found before moving on.
Step 4: Evaluate your top candidates against the criteria from Step 3. Tell me how each one measures up based on what you found and identify which one you are leaning toward and why.
Step 5: Deliver a final recommendation with the agency name, the reasons you selected them over the others, the key questions I should ask them in an introductory call, and any risks I should be aware of going in.

Tweak:
Before finalizing your recommendation in Step 5, ask yourself whether this agency genuinely fits a 180-person company or whether your recommendation is defaulting to size and name recognition. If you cannot find specific evidence that they work well with mid-size clients, flag that gap explicitly rather than glossing over it.
```
